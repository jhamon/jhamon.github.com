About Me

I've got science degrees from MIT and Caltech, so I'm no stranger to hard problems. A year ago, I was a Caltech graduate student using Python and R to analyze geophysical data, and even though that was interesting and important work I found myself falling in love with the programming for its own sake. I realized I wanted the coding to be my full-time focus, so that's what I've been working toward since that time.

In many ways my scientific training was the ideal preparation for a career in technology: I learned how to learn new things quickly, solve problems on my own, and never give up. I'll always carry those lessons with me wherever I go.

Even though I've been programming in various capacities for many years, I've spent thousands of hours over the last several months to level up my skills in all areas with a strong focus on the client-side Javascript and Rails ecosystems of web technologies. As my portfolio shows, I've learned to build modern software for the web and I have to say it's the most fulfilling thing I've ever done.

You can judge the results, and I hope you'll be in touch if your company is looking to expand the size of its dev team.

What I'm Looking For

I want to work with the smartest people who will have me around. I'm hoping to join a company where my skills will be of immediate use, and where I will constantly be challenged to stretch my skills and keep learning.

This probably means a mid-sized, technology-focused company with developers who are pushing the limits of what's possible on the web. I want to work in a company where the people feel passionate about their mission. I want to work among professionals who place value in enforcing best practices during regular code reviews. And I want to work in a company where people are collaborative in their approach to problem solving.

These are the traits of a healthy company where I expect I will have opportunities to contribute and learn the most. If it sounds like I'm describing your company, I'd love to meet you.
